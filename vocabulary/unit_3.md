# 3A - Ideas and Thoughts

| Word | Part of speech | Russian                                    | Example |
|------|----------------|--------------------------------------------|---------|
| occur to | V | прийти в голову                            | It **occurs to me** that we should get out of town for the day. |
| spring to mind | V | быстро приходить в голову                  | I’ve been thinking but nothing **springs to mind**. |
| dawn on | V | внезапно доходить до сознания, озарить     | Something’s just **dawned on me** – we’ve forgotten about Martin! |
| bounce a few ideas around | V | обсудить несколько идей, покидаться идеями | I think we should **bounce a few ideas around** and see what we come up with. |
| bright idea | N | хорошая идея                               | I’ve had a **bright idea** – let’s take our shoes off! |
| passing thought | N | мимолётная мысль                           | OK, relax, we don’t do it – it was just a **passing thought**. |
| out of ideas | A | без идей, идеи закончились                 | I’m afraid I’m **out of ideas** for now. |
| give somebody an idea | V | подать кому-то идею, натолкнуть на мысль   | Seeing that amazing film has **given me an idea** – let’s go shark diving! |
| brainwave | N | блестящая идея, озарение                   | Donny had a **brainwave** about how to get the funding. |


# 3C - News and Fake News

| Word | Part of speech | Russian | Example |
|------|----------------|---------|---------|
| impartial | A | беспристрастный, непредвзятый | Very few newspapers are **impartial**, although they all claim they offer an objective representation. |
| disclose | V | раскрывать, разглашать | The press should never **disclose** sensitive information about ongoing police investigations. |
| sensationalism | N | сенсационность, погоня за сенсацией | There’s too much **sensationalism** in the news at the moment. |
| unsubstantiated claims | N | необоснованные, неподтверждённые утверждения | A lot of the stories we’re seeing are full of **unsubstantiated claims**. |
| fake footage | N | фальшивые видеокадры, поддельная съёмка | I’m sure I could recognise **fake footage** of a news event if I saw it on TV. |
| character assassination | N | очернение репутации, попытка уничтожить репутацию | Many stories about politicians are full of personal attacks and **character assassination** from journalists with opposing views. |
| bend the truth | V | искажать правду, приукрашивать правду | Creators of news want to find an angle to a story, even if it means **bending the truth**. |
| credible | A | правдоподобный, внушающий доверие | Most fake news posts are very **credible**, but some are just ridiculous. |
| publicity stunt | N | рекламный трюк, пиар-ход | Fake news posts and articles are usually just ludicrous **publicity stunts**. |
| interpretation | N | интерпретация, толкование | A lot of what people call fake news could be true – it’s just a question of **interpretation** of the facts. |

# 3D - Summarising and Referring to Information

| Word | Part of speech | Russian | Example |
|------|----------------|---------|---------|
| as follows | A | следующим образом | The main points are **as follows**. |
| the gist of it | N | суть, основная идея | The **gist of it** is that we need more time. |
| in a nutshell | A | кратко, в двух словах | **In a nutshell**, the plan failed because it was too expensive. |
| the former / the latter | N | первый / последний | Of the two options, the **former** is cheaper and the **latter** is faster. |
| not only that, but | A | не только это, но и | The report was late. **Not only that, but** it was inaccurate. |
| the previous section | N | предыдущий раздел | In the **previous section**, we looked at the causes of the problem. |
| the following section | N | следующий раздел | In the **following section**, we will discuss possible solutions. |
| in that case | A | в таком случае | **In that case**, we should wait for more information. |
